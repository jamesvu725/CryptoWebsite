Reverse Alphabet

Reverse Alphabet is a simple substitution cipher where you reverse the alphabet order and each letter in the alphabet is assigned a reversed alphabet letter in the same position. Example: A = Z, B = Y, etc.

Pros:

  • Beginner friendly
  • Easy to create
  • Good for young kids

Cons:

  • Easy to solve

About Giving Hints:

  • Generally unneeded for a simple cryptogram such as this one.
  • Avoid giving a hint such as A = Z since the solver can easily use their intuition to figure it out.

Here is some creative ways to increase the difficulty of Reverse Alphabet:

  • Create a double layered cryptogram by combining it with another cryptogram.
    • Example: Reverse Alphabet combined with Letter to Number
    • Original Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    • Encrypted Message: 7 19 22 / 10 6 18 24 16 / 25 9 12 4 13 / 21 12 3 / 17 6 14 11 8 / 12 5 22 9 / 7 19 22 / 15 26 1 2 / 23 12 20
  • Use it in conjunction with another cryptogram by alternating cryptograms.
    • Example: Letter to Number and Reverse Alphabet
    • Original Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    • Encrypted Message: 20 S 5 / J 21 R 3 P / 2 I 15 D 14 / U 15 C / 10 F 13 K 19 / L 22 V 18 / G 8 V / 12 Z 26 B / 4 L 7
  • Use it to hide a fake message while using it with another cryptogram
    • Example: Letter to Number and Reverse Alphabet
    • Original Message: Real Secret
    • Fake Message: Fake Answer
    • Encrypted Message: 6I 1V 11Z 5O 1H 14V 19X 23I 5V 18G
