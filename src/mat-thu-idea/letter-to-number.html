Letter to Number

There are 26 letters in the alphabet. Each letter is assigned the number corresponding to its position in the alphabet. You substitute each letter in your message with the number.

Example: A = 1, B = 2, etc.

Pros:

  • Beginner friendly
  • Easy to create
  • Good for young kids

Cons:

  • Easy to solve

About Giving Hints:

  • Generally unneeded for a simple cryptogram such as this one.
  • Avoid giving a hint such as A = 1 since the solver can easily use their intuition to figure it out.

Here is some creative ways to increase the difficulty of Letter to Number:

  • Use it in conjunction with another alphabet based cryptogram and replace all the letters with numbers.
    • Example: Anagrams with Numbers
    • Original Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    • Encrypted Message: 8 5 20 / 11 3 21 9 17 / 18 2 15 14 23 / 24 15 6 / 10 13 16 19 21 / 18 22 5 15 / 5 8 20 / 1 26 25 12 / 15 7 4
  • Use it in conjunction with another cryptogram by alternating cryptograms.
    • Example: Letter to Number and Reverse Alphabet
    • Original Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    • Encrypted Message: 20 S 5 / J 21 R 3 P / 2 I 15 D 14 / U 15 C / 10 F 13 K 19 / L 22 V 18 / G 8 V / 12 Z 26 B / 4 L 7
  • Use it to hide a fake message while using it with another cryptogram
    • Example: Letter to Number and Reverse Alphabet
    • Original Message: Real Secret
    • Fake Message: Fake Answer
    • Encrypted Message: 6I 1V 11Z 5O 1H 14V 19X 23I 5V 18G
