Shift Cipher

The shift cipher also known as the Caesar's cipher is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter of the message is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet.

Example: With a shift of 3, A->D

Pros:

  • Easy to solve once you figure it out

Cons:

  • Will be hard to figure out if you are not familiar with it

About Giving Hints:

  • Give at least one hint showing the shift number or the key to a letter such as A = D
  • Giving the shift number makes it easier for the solver while giving a letter makes it harder to find the relationship

First, choose a shift number between 1 and 25. Count down the alphabet that many time. The letter you stop at is now the start of your shifted alphabet. Replace all the letters in your message using the shifted alphabet.

Example:

Shift number = 3. Three down the alphabet is D. D is the start of the shifted alphabet. The letter A in the original message is now D in the encrypted message. The letter B is now E and so forth.

Original Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.

Encrypted Message: WKH TXLFN EURZQ IRA MXPSV RYHU WKH ODCB GRJ

Input the shift number you want for your new alphabet. Please input an integer from 1 to 25.

First, determine what your shift number is. If you are not given the shift number but have the relationship between two letters, convert both letters to numbers and subtract them. If the difference of the two number is negative, add 26 to the difference. Shift the alphabet backwards and replace all the letters in your message with the shifted alphabet. If the shift is wrong, try 26 minus your number instead.

Example:

You are given D = A as your hint. D = 4 and A = 1. The difference between the two numbers is 3. Your shift number is 3.

Encrypted Message: WKH TXLFN EURZQ IRA MXPSV RYHU WKH ODCB GRJ

Decrypted Message: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
